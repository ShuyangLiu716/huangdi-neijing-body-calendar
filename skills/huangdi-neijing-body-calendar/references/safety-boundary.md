# Safety Boundary

This skill provides wellness education and self-check prompts. It does not provide clinical diagnosis, prescriptions, treatment plans, or emergency triage.

## Always Avoid

- Diagnosing a disease or syndrome from user symptoms.
- Prescribing Chinese herbal formulas, single herbs, medicated diet formulas, acupuncture points, moxibustion, cupping, scraping, massage techniques for disease treatment, or medication changes.
- Claiming that exercise, diet, sleep, or emotion regulation can cure or prevent a disease.
- Recommending high-intensity exercise when the user reports unexplained symptoms, acute illness, recent injury, pregnancy complications, or unstable chronic disease.

## Red Flags

If any appear, recommend stopping strenuous activity and seeking timely medical care or emergency help depending on severity:

- Chest pain, chest tightness, severe shortness of breath, fainting, near-fainting, or irregular heartbeat during rest or activity.
- Sudden severe headache, one-sided weakness/numbness, facial droop, speech difficulty, confusion, or vision loss.
- Severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, blood in stool or vomit, black stool, or unexplained severe dehydration.
- High fever, stiff neck, severe infection signs, or rapidly worsening condition.
- Acute injury with deformity, inability to bear weight, major swelling, severe pain, numbness, or loss of function.
- New or worsening pain that wakes the user at night, follows trauma, or is accompanied by fever, unexplained weight loss, or neurological symptoms.
- Pregnancy with bleeding, severe abdominal pain, severe headache, dizziness/fainting, chest symptoms, or reduced fetal movement.

## Exercise Caution

Use low-intensity guidance or recommend professional assessment when the user mentions:

- Known cardiovascular, pulmonary, kidney, metabolic, neurological, or musculoskeletal disease.
- Recent surgery, hospitalization, infection, concussion, fracture, or medication change.
- New unexplained symptoms during exercise.
- Older adult frailty, fall risk, or very low baseline activity.
- Children, adolescents, pregnancy, postpartum, or chronic pain.

## Safe Response Pattern

For risk prompts, answer in this order:

1. State the boundary: this is not a diagnosis.
2. Identify whether there are red flags based on what the user said.
3. If red flags are possible, advise medical evaluation before exercise or self-care escalation.
4. Give only conservative, general actions: rest, stop painful activity, gentle walking or mobility if tolerated, hydration, regular sleep.
5. Invite clinically relevant follow-up questions only if they change immediate safety guidance.

## Knee Pain Example

Do:

- "今天先避免跑跳、深蹲负重和疼痛动作。若无明显肿胀、卡住、无法负重或外伤，可试 10-20 分钟平地慢走或轻柔活动度练习，以不加重疼痛为准。若疼痛持续、肿胀或影响走路，建议就医评估。"

Do not:

- "这是寒湿痹阻，用某方治疗。"
- "按某穴位每天艾灸即可恢复。"
- "继续训练把它练开。"
